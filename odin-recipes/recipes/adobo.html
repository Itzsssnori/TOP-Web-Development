Philippine Adobo

Description

Philippine adobo (from Spanish: adobar: "marinade", "sauce" or "seasoning" / English: /əˈdoʊboʊ/, Tagalog pronunciation: [ɐdobo]) is a popular Filipino dish and cooking process in Philippine cuisine. In its base form, meat, seafood, or vegetables are first browned in oil, and then marinated and simmered in vinegar, salt and/or soy sauce, and garlic. It is often considered the unofficial national dish in the Philippines

Ingredients

  • Meat (beef, chicken, pork)
  • Seafood/Vegetables
  • Soy sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Cooking oil
  • Garlic
  • Black peppercorn
  • Bay leaf

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken: In a large bowl or container, combine the chicken with the soy sauce and half of the garlic. Cover and let it marinate for at least 20 minutes (up to a few hours in the fridge for deeper flavor).
  2. Sear the chicken: Heat oil in a large pot or heavy-bottomed Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade (reserve the marinade) and sear until the skin is nicely browned on both sides. Remove the chicken and set aside.
  3. Sauté the aromatics: In the remaining oil, sauté the remaining garlic until fragrant and lightly golden (don't burn it).
  4. Simmer and braise: Return the chicken to the pot and pour in the reserved marinade, water, bay leaves, and peppercorns. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and let it simmer for about 20 to 30 minutes until the chicken is tender.
  5. Add the vinegar: Stir in the vinegar and optional sugar. Let it simmer uncovered for another 5–10 minutes to cook out the harsh acidity and thicken the sauce.
  6. Serve: Serve hot over a bed of steamed white rice.
