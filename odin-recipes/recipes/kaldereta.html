Kaldereta

Description

Kaldereta or caldereta is a meat stew from the Philippines. Variations of the dish use beef,chicken,pork, or goat. The meat is usually stewed with vegetables and liver paste and spiced with red chillies. The vegetables may include tomatoes, potatoes, olives, bell peppers, and hot peppers. Kaldereta sometimes includes tomato sauce. It is an everyday dish in the Philippines that is also served during festivities and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • Meat chuck or brisket (cut into cubes)
  • Cloves garlic, large onion
  • Soy sauce
  • Tomato sauce
  • Cooking oil, dried bay leaves
  • Soy sauce, potatoes, carrots
  • Red & green bell pepper
  • Bay leaf

Steps

  1. Sear the Meat: Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Season the beef with a little salt and pepper, and sear in batches until browned on all sides. Remove the beef and set aside.
  2. Fry the Veggies: In the same pot, fry the cubed potatoes and carrots until the edges slightly brown (about 3–5 minutes). Remove and set aside.
  3. Sauté the aromatics: Reduce heat to medium. In the same pot, sauté the garlic, onions, and chili until the onions are soft and translucent.
  4. Build the Stew: Return the beef to the pot. Stir in the liver spread, soy sauce, and tomato sauce, cooking for about 2 minutes.
  5. Simmer: Add the bay leaves and pour in the beef broth. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat to a gentle simmer. Cover and cook for 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the beef is fork-tender (add more water/broth if the pot gets too dry).
  6. Add Vegetables: Once the meat is tender, add the fried potatoes, carrots, and the sliced bell peppers. Simmer for 10–15 minutes until the vegetables are soft.
  7. Thicken & Finish: Stir in the grated cheese and green olives. Let it simmer for 5 more minutes until the cheese melts into the sauce, giving it a rich, thick consistency. Season with salt and pepper to taste, then serve hot with white rice.
