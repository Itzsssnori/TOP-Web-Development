Filipino-style Fried Chicken

Description

Filipino-style fried chicken is characterized by a distinctive marinade of soy sauce, citrus (calamansi or lemon), garlic, and black pepper, yielding a savory, tangy, and juicy flavor profile. It is often breaded using a mix of flour and cornstarch for a crispy crust and is traditionally served with rice and banana ketchup.

Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Calamansi juice
  • Soy sauce
  • Fish sauce (patis)
  • Minced garlic
  • Magic Sarap/Knorr Liquid
  • Black pepper
  • Bread and Coat

Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken: In a large bowl, combine the calamansi juice, soy sauce, garlic, salt, and pepper. Add the chicken and ensure it is fully coated. Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, or overnight for deeper flavor.
  2. Prepare the Breading Mixture: In a shallow dish or bowl, whisk together the all-purpose flour, cornstarch, and baking powder.
  3. Coat the Chicken: Remove the chicken from the marinade and let the excess liquid drip off. For extra crunch: Dip the chicken pieces into the beaten egg, then dredge and press firmly into the flour mixture.
  4. Fry the Chicken: Heat the cooking oil in a wide, deep-bottomed pan or wok over medium heat until the temperature reaches 350F to 375F. Fry the chicken in batches so the pan is not overcrowded. Cook for 12 to 15 minutes, turning halfway through, until golden brown and the internal temperature reaches 165F.
  5. Drain and Serve: Transfer to a wire rack or a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil. Serve immediately alongside steamed white rice with banana ketchup or your favorite homemade gravy.
